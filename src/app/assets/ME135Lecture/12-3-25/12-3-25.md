## Integral Momentum Method for Laminar Boundary Layer on a Flat Plate

**Lecture Type:** derivation-heavy

### Summary
This lecture introduces the integral momentum method to analyze a laminar boundary layer developing on a flat plate. It explains how mass and momentum balances applied to a control volume enclosing the boundary layer lead to the momentum integral equation, defines the momentum thickness as a characteristic integral measure of the profile, and shows how to obtain wall shear and skin-friction coefficients by combining the integral result with the viscous shear at the wall. An example using a linear velocity profile is worked through to obtain scaling laws for boundary-layer thickness and local/average skin-friction behavior with Reynolds number.

### Core Topics
- Integral momentum method
- Momentum integral equation
- Momentum thickness (θ)
- Mass and momentum balances for boundary layer
- Wall shear stress (τw) and viscous shear
- Local skin-friction coefficient (Cf)
- Scaling of boundary-layer thickness with x and Re_x
- Example: linear velocity profile approximation
- Average skin-friction over a plate

### Learning Objectives
1. Explain the rationale and setup of the integral momentum method for boundary layers
2. Define and interpret the momentum thickness and its physical meaning
3. Apply mass and momentum balances to derive the momentum integral relation for a control volume
4. Compute wall shear stress from the integral momentum balance and from viscous gradient at the wall
5. Use a prescribed velocity profile to solve for boundary-layer thickness growth with streamwise distance
6. Derive scaling laws for local and average skin-friction coefficients and express their Reynolds-number dependence

### Assumed Prerequisites
- Basic fluid mechanics (conservation of mass and momentum)
- Boundary-layer fundamentals (concept of velocity profile and no-slip)
- Elementary calculus and integration
- Concept of Reynolds number and kinematic viscosity


## Derivations

### **Integral momentum method — momentum thickness and wall shear**
**Stub:** Derive the integral momentum balance for a flat-plate boundary layer to obtain F = ρ W u_∞^2 θ(x), the relation τ_w(x) = ρ u_∞^2 dθ/dx and (by equating with viscous shear) the boundary-layer growth δ(x) and local skin-friction coefficient C_f(x).

**Steps:**
1. Geometry and control volume: consider a thin rectangular control volume spanning width W in spanwise direction, from y=0 (wall) to y=δ(x) (edge of BL). Free-stream speed is u_∞.
2. Mass balance for the control volume (inflow at AB, outflow at CD and BC): $$\dot m_{AB}-\dot m_{CD}-\dot m_{BC}=0 \Rightarrow \dot m_{BC}=\dot m_{AB}-\dot m_{CD}.$$
3. Express mass flow rates: $$\dot m_{AB}=\rho (\delta W) u_\infty,\qquad \dot m_{CD}=\int_0^{\delta} \rho W u(y)\,dy.$$
4. Therefore the mass flux through BC (the dashed slanted face) is $$\dot m_{BC}=\rho W\bigg( u_\infty\int_0^{\delta}dy - \int_0^{\delta} u(y)\,dy\bigg)=\rho W u_\infty\int_0^{\delta}\big(1-\frac{u(y)}{u_\infty}\big)dy.$$
5. Momentum balance for the control volume in x-direction (steady): $$\dot M_{AB}-\dot M_{CD}-\dot M_{BC}-F=0.$$
6. Write the momentum flux terms: $$\dot M_{AB}=\rho u_\infty^2 W\delta,\qquad \dot M_{CD}=\int_0^{\delta}\rho W u^2(y)\,dy,$$ and using $\dot M_{BC}=u_\infty\dot m_{BC}$ gives $$\dot M_{BC}=\rho W u_\infty^2\int_0^{\delta}\Big(1-\frac{u(y)}{u_\infty}\Big)dy.$$
7. Solve for the force (momentum deficit) F on the control volume (equal and opposite to shear on the plate over section): combine terms and simplify: $$F=\dot M_{AB}-\dot M_{CD}-\dot M_{BC}=\rho W\int_0^{\delta}\big(u_\infty u(y)-u^2(y)\big)dy.$$
8. Define momentum thickness $\theta(x)$ and express F compactly: $$\theta(x)=\int_0^{\delta}\frac{u(y)}{u_\infty}\Big(1-\frac{u(y)}{u_\infty}\Big)dy,\qquad F=\rho W u_\infty^2\theta(x).$$
9. Relate local shear to derivative of F: for a differential streamwise slice, $dF = W\,\tau_w(x)\,dx$, so $$\tau_w(x)=\frac{1}{W}\frac{dF}{dx}=\rho u_\infty^2\frac{d\theta}{dx}.$$
10. Viscous shear (near-wall) from gradient: $$\tau_w=\mu\left.\frac{du}{dy}\right|_{y=0}.$$ Equate the two expressions for $\tau_w$ to obtain the integral differential relation linking velocity profile (via θ) and viscous gradient: $$\rho u_\infty^2\frac{d\theta}{dx}=\mu\left.\frac{du}{dy}\right|_{y=0}.$$
11. Completeness: the derivation up to the general relation $$\tau_w(x)=\rho u_\infty^2\frac{d\theta}{dx}$$ and the definition of $\theta(x)$ is complete on pages 1–3. The step equating to viscous shear is shown but solution of the resulting ODE requires an assumed profile (see supporting derivation: linear-profile example).

### **Mass-flux expression for the slanted face (ṁ_BC)**
**Stub:** Compute mass flow through the slanted outflow BC and express it in integral form.

**Steps:**
1. Starting from the mass-balance relation: $$\dot m_{BC}=\dot m_{AB}-\dot m_{CD}.$$
2. Substitute: $$\dot m_{AB}=\rho u_\infty(\delta W),\qquad \dot m_{CD}=\int_0^{\delta}\rho W u(y)\,dy.$$
3. Hence $$\dot m_{BC}=\rho W u_\infty\int_0^{\delta}\Big(1-\frac{u(y)}{u_\infty}\Big)dy.$$
4. This supporting result is used in the momentum flux term $\dot M_{BC}=u_\infty\dot m_{BC}$ in the main momentum balance.

### **Linear-profile example — compute θ(x), δ(x) and C_f(x)**
**Stub:** Assume a linear boundary-layer profile u(y)=u_∞(y/δ) to compute θ=δ/6, solve for δ(x) using τ_w (both integral and viscous forms) and obtain local skin-friction C_f(x).

**Steps:**
1. Assumed velocity profile: $$u(y)=u_\infty\frac{y}{\delta(x)}\quad(0\le y\le\delta).$$
2. Compute momentum thickness: $$\theta(x)=\int_0^{\delta}\frac{u}{u_\infty}\Big(1-\frac{u}{u_\infty}\Big)dy=\int_0^{\delta}\Big(\frac{y}{\delta}-\frac{y^2}{\delta^2}\Big)dy=\frac{1}{6}\,\delta(x).$$
3. Use integral relation for shear: $$\tau_w=\rho u_\infty^2\frac{d\theta}{dx}=\rho u_\infty^2\frac{1}{6}\frac{d\delta}{dx}.$$
4. Viscous (near-wall) shear from profile: $$\tau_w=\mu\left.\frac{du}{dy}\right|_{y=0}=\mu\frac{u_\infty}{\delta}.$$
5. Equate the two expressions for τ_w: $$\rho u_\infty^2\frac{1}{6}\frac{d\delta}{dx}=\mu\frac{u_\infty}{\delta}.$$ Cancel one u_∞ and rearrange: $$\delta\frac{d\delta}{dx}=\frac{6\mu}{\rho u_\infty}=\frac{6\nu}{u_\infty}.$$
6. Integrate from x=0 with δ(0)=0: $$\frac{1}{2}\delta^2=\frac{6\nu}{u_\infty}x\quad\Rightarrow\quad\delta^2=\frac{12\nu x}{u_\infty}.$$ Equivalently $$\Big(\frac{\delta}{x}\Big)^2=\frac{12}{Re_x},\quad Re_x=\frac{u_\infty x}{\nu}.$$
7. Local skin-friction coefficient from viscous shear: $$\tau_w=\mu\frac{u_\infty}{\delta}=\frac{\mu u_\infty}{\delta}$$ and $$C_f(x)=\frac{2\tau_w}{\rho u_\infty^2}=\frac{2\mu}{\rho u_\infty\,\delta}.$$
8. Substitute δ from above to get the algebraic Reynolds-number dependence: $$C_f(x)=\frac{2}{\sqrt{12}}Re_x^{-1/2}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}Re_x^{-1/2}\quad(\text{for linear profile}).$$
9. Completeness: this example completes the integral relation by assuming a velocity shape; coefficients differ from Blasius solution because of the linear-profile assumption. (Pages 4–5).

### **Relation between F, τ_w(x) and θ(x); expression for τ_w(x)**
**Stub:** Relate total momentum deficit force F over a plate segment to momentum thickness and obtain local shear via differentiation: τ_w(x)=ρ u_∞^2 dθ/dx.

**Steps:**
1. From main momentum result: $$F(x)=\rho W u_\infty^2\theta(x)\quad\text{(force over width W up to x)}.$$
2. Differential relation for local shear: for elemental length dx, $$dF=W\tau_w(x)\,dx\Rightarrow\tau_w(x)=\frac{1}{W}\frac{dF}{dx}=\rho u_\infty^2\frac{d\theta}{dx}.$$
3. This is boxed on the notes and used to equate against viscous shear to get evolution of θ (or δ).
4. Reference: derivation and boxed identity appear on pages 2–3.

### **Average skin-friction coefficient over plate length (sketch)**
**Stub:** Compute average C̄_f over length L using local C_f(x) ~ Re_x^{-1/2} dependence and integrate.

**Steps:**
1. Local form (from example): $$C_f(x)=K\,Re_x^{-1/2},\quad Re_x=\frac{u_\infty x}{\nu}.$$
2. Average over 0..L: $$\overline{C_f}=\frac{1}{L}\int_0^L C_f(x)\,dx=\frac{K}{L}\int_0^L\Big(\frac{u_\infty x}{\nu}\Big)^{-1/2}dx =\frac{K}{L}\Big(\frac{\nu}{u_\infty}\Big)^{1/2}\int_0^L x^{-1/2}dx.$$
3. Evaluate integral: $$\int_0^L x^{-1/2}dx=2L^{1/2},$$ so $$\overline{C_f}=\frac{2K}{\sqrt{L}}\Big(\frac{\nu}{u_\infty}\Big)^{1/2}=2K\,(u_\infty L/\nu)^{-1/2}=2K\,Re_L^{-1/2}.$$
4. The notes perform this integration for their K and obtain the numerical prefactor for the assumed profile (pages 5–6).

## Conceptual Questions### **Conceptual Question**
**Question:** What is the definition of the momentum thickness θ(x) for a boundary layer and what physical quantity does it represent?

**Topics:** Momentum thickness, Boundary layer integral method, Physical interpretation of boundary-layer integrals

**Options:**
- ✅ θ(x)=∫_0^δ (u/u_∞)(1−u/u_∞) dy; it measures the loss of streamwise momentum due to the boundary layer (equivalent momentum deficit).
- θ(x)=∫_0^δ (1−u/u_∞) dy; it is the same as the displacement thickness and measures the displaced mass flux.
- θ(x)=∫_0^δ u dy; it represents the total mass flow inside the boundary layer.
- θ(x)=δ/2 for any velocity profile; it is just half the boundary-layer thickness.

**Explanation:** By definition θ(x)=∫_0^δ (u/u_∞)(1−u/u_∞) dy. It quantifies the momentum deficit (loss of streamwise momentum) caused by the boundary layer relative to the outer flow, not the displaced mass (which is displacement thickness).

 ### **Conceptual Question**
**Question:** Using the integral momentum balance for a control volume enclosing the boundary layer, which of the following relations between wall shear τ_w(x) and momentum thickness θ(x) is correct for an incompressible steady flow with outer speed u_∞?

**Topics:** Integral momentum balance, Wall shear stress, Momentum thickness derivative

**Options:**
- ✅ τ_w(x)=ρ u_∞^2 (dθ/dx)
- τ_w(x)=ρ u_∞ (dθ/dx)
- τ_w(x)=μ (d^2θ/dx^2)
- τ_w(x)=ρ (dθ/dx) / u_∞

**Explanation:** From the integral momentum equation one obtains F (force from shear) = ρ W u_∞^2 dθ/dx, so per unit area τ_w = ρ u_∞^2 dθ/dx. This relates the streamwise change of momentum thickness to the wall shear. Note factors of u_∞^2 and density are essential.

 ### **Conceptual Question**
**Question:** If you assume a simple linear velocity profile u(y)=u_∞(y/δ) inside the boundary layer and equate τ_w from the integral momentum relation with the viscous shear τ_w=μ(du/dy)|_{y=0}, what scaling for δ(x) with streamwise distance x and local Reynolds number Re_x=u_∞ x/ν results?

**Topics:** Boundary-layer growth scaling, Linear profile example, Relation between viscous shear and integral method

**Options:**
- ✅ δ(x) ∝ x^{1/2} Re_x^{-1/2} (i.e. δ ∝ √(ν x / u_∞) )
- δ(x) ∝ x (linear growth with x)
- δ(x) is independent of x and is constant
- δ(x) ∝ x^{2} Re_x^{-1}

**Explanation:** For the linear profile θ=δ/6 and τ_w from the integral method gives τ_w∝ρ u_∞^2 d(δ)/dx. Equating this to τ_w=μ u_∞/δ yields a differential relation whose solution gives δ^2 ∝ ν x / u_∞, so δ∝x^{1/2} and in terms of local Reynolds number (Re_x=u_∞ x/ν) δ/x ∝ Re_x^{-1/2} (i.e. δ∝√(ν x / u_∞)).
